# 📊 Beyond CIBIL: Understanding Credit Behaviour in Tier-2 & Tier-3 India

> A behavioural analysis of **50 anonymized retail credit bureau profiles** to understand borrower behaviour, repayment patterns, underwriting observations and fintech opportunities.

---

# Project Overview

Traditional retail lending often relies heavily on CIBIL scores.

This project explores whether **borrower behaviour** can provide additional underwriting insights beyond the bureau score.

The analysis focuses on identifying behavioural patterns observed across 50 anonymized retail credit profiles from Tier-2 & Tier-3 India.

---

# Objective

The objective of this project is to analyse:

- Credit Score Distribution
- Credit Vintage
- Loan Product Mix
- Borrowing Behaviour
- Repayment Patterns
- Credit Enquiries
- Secured vs Unsecured Lending
- Underwriting Observations
- Behavioural Risk Patterns
- Fintech Opportunities

---

# Dataset

**Profiles Analysed:** 50

**Region:** Tier-2 & Tier-3 India

**Data Used**

- Credit Bureau Information
- Credit Score
- Credit Vintage
- Active Loans
- Loan Mix
- DPD History
- Settlements
- Write-offs
- Recent Credit Enquiries

---

# Methodology

The analysis focuses on identifying behavioural trends instead of evaluating individual borrowers.

Each profile was reviewed using:

- Credit Score
- Repayment Behaviour
- Credit Vintage
- Product Mix
- Active Loan Portfolio
- Secured vs Unsecured Exposure
- Underwriting Assessment

---

# Key Insights

✔ Similar CIBIL scores can represent very different borrower behaviour.

✔ Credit vintage provides valuable underwriting context.

✔ Product mix helps explain borrowing preferences.

✔ Behavioural analysis complements traditional underwriting.

✔ Repayment history provides insights beyond credit score alone.
